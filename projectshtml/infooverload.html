Project: Information
Overload

What is the fundamental cause of error?


This project approaches error as a condition that emerges when too many elements accumulate continuously and simultaneously. Contemporary interfaces are structured by constant notifications, layered information systems, and multiple streams of content consumed at once. Within this environment, user attention is persistently fragmented, and information becomes increasingly difficult to process.


Rather than explaining this condition, the video is designed for the viewer to experience it directly. Visual elements and text progressively overlap, accelerate, and distort. As informational density increases, clarity diminishes. What once functioned as a signal transforms into noise.
