Ayesha Thakur


Project: A Book on
Stewardship, Rupture,
and Consequence






This project is a book that explores the human relationship with the earth, structured in three parts. The first part, Care and Responsibility, brings together sources from Indigenous and religious texts to highlight the role of humans as caretakers of the earth. It focuses on responsibility, balance, and coexistence, showing how humans are meant to live in relation to land, animals, and the environment.


The second part, The Relationship Was Abandoned, examines how humans moved away from these roles of care and coexistence. It looks at how colonization, industrialization, and extractive practices replaced responsibility with control, leading to harm across land, animals, and ecosystems. This section reflects the breakdown of relationships that once supported balance and sustainability.


The third part focuses on the lasting consequences of this shift. Instead of written research, it relies solely on images to show environmental damage across land and water. The photographs, drawn from the works of Edward Burtynsky and the Anthropocene Project, highlight the scale of destruction caused by extraction, industry, and neglect, emphasizing that this damage is ongoing rather than something confined to the past.


Overall, the project frames the Anthropocene as a result of humans abandoning their responsibility and stewardship, presenting environmental harm as continuous rather than accidental.
