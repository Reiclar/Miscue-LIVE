Project: PiDGN QUEST

PiDGN Quest is an anti-game reliant on the human need for completion that it never seeks to fulfill. Through uncompletable levels, missing code, and a character created to capture the user's empathy and keep them motivated to continue playing, the project leaves the user feeling defeated and helpless, unaware that the game can only end in failure.


Created for OCAD U’s Graphic Design 4 course, PiDGN QUEST was created using the idea of incompleteness and relies on the Zeigarnik Effect’ highlighting how the human brain continuously seeks closure. When a task is left incomplete, the individual experiences a state of psychological tension, keeping the task active in their memory until completion is achieved. This, as it chases completion, it functions as an endless error, feeding off of a human’s perception of their own shortcomings.
