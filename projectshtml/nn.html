Project: Nakaka Nosebleed

Nakaka Nosebleed is an audiovisual representation of the linguistic relationship between Filipinos who were raised in the homeland and Filipinos who were raised abroad using a collective sense of error. Through a video narrative and a flipbook, the project offers a familiar experience to second-generation Filipino North Americans, or a new perspective of inunderstanding for those who seek it. The narrative connects the colonization of the Philippines to its present-day consequence of cultural distance that both sides are ashamed of. Here, error is present in the destruction of a people, culture, loss, and in understanding.


One of the declarations that Philippine hero José Rizal held with conviction was this: “When a people holds onto its language, it holds onto a semblance of freedom, like a man who holds onto his independence when he retains his own way of thinking. Language is the thought of a people.” Nakaka Nosebleed is a reflection of language lost, and the lack of freedom through the limits of communication. If language is the thought of a people, then this project speaks one unfinished due to the struggle to express it. They are fragmented thoughts for a fragmented identity.
